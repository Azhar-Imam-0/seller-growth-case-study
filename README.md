# 📈 Seller Growth Case Study

> A real-world case study documenting the decision-making framework I used to manage and optimize high-spending D2C seller accounts.

---

# Overview

Managing seller growth extends beyond running advertising campaigns. Every optimization decision should contribute to improving business profitability.

This repository explains the structured workflow I followed while managing 200+ seller accounts, beginning with Meta Ads performance analysis and ending with overall seller profitability.

# Step 1 – Meta Ads Performance Review

Every optimization cycle begins with reviewing campaign performance.

## Metrics Reviewed

| Metric | Why It Matters | Common Optimization |
|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| CPA | Cost per purchase | Audience, creatives, bidding |
| CPP | Purchase efficiency | Compare campaign profitability |
| CPM | Auction cost | Audience quality, competition |
| CTR | Creative engagement | Refresh creatives and messaging |
| CPC | Traffic cost | Improve ad relevance |
| CVR | Website conversion | Landing page, pricing, offers |
| AOV | Revenue per order | Bundles, pricing strategy |
| ROAS | Overall marketing profitability | Determines scaling decisions |

---

# Step 2 – Identify Problem Metrics

Before increasing budgets, I identify which metric is limiting campaign performance.

Example:

```
ROAS ↓

↓

CPA High

↓

Check Audience
Check Creative
Check Budget
Check Placement

↓

Optimize

↓

Review Again
```

The goal is to fix the underlying issue rather than reacting only to the final ROAS.

---

# Step 3 – Campaign Optimization

Depending on the diagnosed issue, optimization actions may include:

- Audience refinement
- Creative refresh
- Budget redistribution
- Placement optimization
- Bid strategy review
- Product-level optimization
- Pricing recommendations

---

# Step 4 – Scaling Decision

Campaigns are considered for scaling only after maintaining stable performance.

### Scaling Checklist

- Healthy CPA
- Stable ROAS
- Good CTR
- Consistent Purchase Volume
- Positive Conversion Rate
- Budget availability

Only profitable campaigns are scaled.

---

# Step 5 – Business Performance Review

Marketing success must translate into business success.

After campaign optimization, I review:

## GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)

- Revenue generated
- Sales trend
- Product performance

## NMV (Net Merchandise Value)

Used to evaluate actual business value after accounting for returns and operational costs.

---

# Step 6 – Seller Profitability Analysis

Marketing metrics alone don't explain overall profitability.

Every seller account is reviewed using operational KPIs.

| Metric | Business Impact | Typical Action |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Seller Cancellation | Lost fulfilled orders | Inventory and operational review |
| Customer Cancellation | Reduced realized revenue | Product quality, pricing, delivery review |
| RTO | Logistics loss | Confirmation process, shipping review |
| Incorrect COGS | Lower profitability | Validate cost inputs |
| High Logistics Charges | Margin reduction | Courier and shipping optimization |
| Marketing Cost | Direct profit impact | Campaign optimization |

---

# Step 7 – Learning Limited Diagnosis

One of the most common Meta Ads delivery issues is **Learning Limited**.

When campaigns remain in Learning Limited, purchase volume often drops, delivery becomes unstable, and CPA starts increasing.

## Symptoms

- Orders drop significantly
- Inconsistent spend
- Increasing CPA
- Declining ROAS
- Limited delivery

## Optimization Checklist for Learning Limited

### 1. Review Purchase Volume (Last 7 Days)

Check whether the campaign is generating enough optimization events.

---

### 2. Compare CPA with Daily Budget

Example:

Daily Budget = ₹2,000

CPA = ₹900

Expected Purchases ≈ 2/day

This is generally insufficient for Meta to gather enough optimization signals.

---

### 3. Review Campaign Structure

If too many small ad sets compete for the same audience:

- Merge overlapping ad sets
- Simplify campaign structure
- Increase available learning signals

---

### 4. Review Audience

If targeting is overly restrictive:

- Broaden targeting
- Expand audience size
- Use Advantage+ Audience where appropriate

---

### 5. Review Creative Performance

Analyze:

- CTR
- CPC
- Conversion Rate
- Frequency

If performance indicates creative fatigue:

- Launch new creatives
- Refresh messaging
- Test additional formats

---

### 6. Avoid Frequent Edits

After implementing the necessary optimizations:

- Avoid unnecessary edits for several days
- Allow Meta's algorithm to stabilize
- Monitor recovery before making additional changes
